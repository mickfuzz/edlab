The 3 Rs: Reflecting, Redesigning & Representing EdLab Experiences

Our 3 Rs session is designed to give EdLab students the chance to reflect, redesign and represent their EdLab experience.

Reflection was facilitated by discussing EdLab projects with the group. Within this session we had students who had worked on an education pack for a local theatre company, a science and poetry session for year 6 children, a half term event teaching coding through music and a proposed project for families in the library.


In order to help students to redesign their EdLab experience we experimented with some gamification of learning. Gamification of learning is a way in which to engage learners in enjoyable and enriching activities (see Kim & Lee 2015). We did this by creating a computer game like proforma to facilitate articulation of ideas and concrete next tasks for developing these ideas. As well as providing space for some ‘blue sky thinking’ about aspirational projects in the future.

An Agile approach to planning your project

You’ve come up with your project ideas, you know the Why, What, When, Who and Where. Now what do you do next? How do you change your ideas into reality?

One of the problems about trying to manage a project is Murphy’s Law which states that whatever can go wrong will go wrong. On the other hand the alternative of struggling ahead with no plan is clearly not a great alternative. Another issue about planning systems is that they can take you away from what you are actually doing and draw you into a world of charts and timelines.

To try to avoid these problems we can try an agile approach to project work. In this post I want to focus on a few features of being agile and look at a tool and simple framework that may help.
GANNT charts which are often used by project managers to plan our projects years in advance. Let’s try a different approach.

Practicalities – Online and Offline

You can use a physical print out and post it notes to plan in this way. If you want to collaborate online you can use a Card based project management tool like Trello. It’s free so sign up and try it out.

Our projects are different. We all have an input into what happens and we need a system which isn’t a pain to update. This A3 Agile project planner is also available as an SVG file here.

Timeboxes

Timeboxes are an area of a chart that relate to when you will do each of the tasks within them. Our advice here is to keep things simple. In our model there are three main boxes where your tasks go, Current Sprint, Next Up and On Hold.

Current Sprint: these are tasks that need to happen by the next time you meet. Your sprint duration may be a week. It might get shorter as you get closer to the event and you need to meet up more often to get more done.

Next Up: The jobs which you know will need to be done next. These may be in order of priority to make sure you don’t get held up in the future.

On Hold: Tasks which you should be allocating now but for some reason are held up. This may be because you don’t have the information you need for example.

Breakdown your tasks

Especially in your sprint period every task should have an allocated person to lead on it even if it will be done by more than one person.
If it makes sense then put down the day the job should be done.

Breakdown your bigger jobs into lots of little tasks. A task shouldn’t take longer than 15 min. If it does, then break it up into different ones. Can these different tasks be given out to more than one person?

Keeping communication going is also a job so don’t forget to put down jobs like:

  • Ring around group to check status of tasks and that people have what they need
  • Set a meeting time next Tues or Wed and invite everyone
  • Make sure all tasks this sprint have a person allocated

Be Flexible

Don’t fear the on hold box. Our approach to projects are all about adapting to what is happening. If something is On Hold then let’s use this as a way of finding out what we need to change to make our project go forward.

There are two other boxes that can help us to move things out of the On Hold box. Maybe we need to look at our overall aims of the project. The Why, What, Who, Where and When. If we can’t make progress doing promotion, tt could be we need to change the audience. If there are things we don’t know about he venue maybe we need to do it in a different place.

Remember *you* are the project

An agile approach should focus on the people involved. It’s you as a group that have the ideas and skills that will solve your challenges. If your plan is distracting from that then take a step back. Have a conversation, have a cup of tea, have disputes but then find your common ground and agree to your tasks. Think of each task as a small step towards a greater goal

Never doubt that a small group of thoughtful, committed citizens can change the world; indeed, it’s the only thing that ever has.
Margaret Mead

Y6 Pupils at Manchester Met Learning about Science through Poetry and Poetry through Science

This week EdLab worked with students and staff to challenge the boundaries between science and poetry with Y6 pupils from Rolls Crescent primary school. We worked with Manchester Met’s Science Communication lecturer Dr Sam Illingworth to run a creative education workshop mixing these diverse subject areas. The pupils were excited and enthusiastic about the opportunity and teacher Juliet Lanyon said that it was a ‘privilege’ to be able to bring pupils into Manchester Met. Further, the children loved working with ‘Dr Sam’, as they called him, and in poetic style, described their participation as ‘fizzplosive’ and ‘awesome.’


Run Science and Poetry Workshops


This was an ideal project for our students because the aim of EdLab is to be creative and innovative with education, through offering activities to local communities whilst also building our students’ employability. The EdLab students praised the event, and were enthusiastic about the opportunity to apply their knowledge to practical activities. Seren Ruane said, ‘Today I was given the wonderful opportunity to work with children with EAL, which will help my development as a primary trainee school teacher.’


This was a positive and valuable experience for pupils and students, and that it is the start of a project which will be developed further by Edlab students in order to offer more children the chance to be innovative with scientific experiments and creative writing.

Birley Community Day: Mapping Local Learning

Yesterday, the EdLab team had the pleasure of meeting lots of people from our local area at the Birley Community Day hosted by MMU in Hulme. As a project about engaging students with the local community and informal learning in enterprising ways, we wanted to know more about people’s local learning experiences. After managing to print a big map of the area in our lovely Art department, we were set, but we needed local people to talk to us. Luckily, either our charm, or our free marshmallows brought them in!


People were really great in telling us their experiences of learning around Hulme. Chloe told us that she learnt about Egypt at her Primary school, Elena learned to swim, Bekki found out she was pregnant, and Mary Williams learned to teach tie dye classes. Everyone told us about the places where they lived, the places they knew, and the places that were gone. For example, David was concerned about the loss of learning support in local libraries, and Ali was sad about the closing of adult education classes. Others however, told us about the work going on at the William Griffiths centre, Venture Arts and family centres in the area which they valued greatly.

Overall we were excited by the opportunities present in our local community, and the enthusiasm of people to work with MMU students through EdLab, to create and enhance learning opportunities and experiences. Now we must live up to our community!

EdLab has Launched!

So today we launched EdLab, and joined some of our shiny new students with real live educational challenges! The lovely people from Hulme Garden Centre popped across the road, Paul from Z-Arts walked up the street, Liz from Code Club demonstrated some musical bananas and The Hive came down to see what was occurring.


It was great to start learning about what our students are interested in, what their #goodstuff is. Many students were excited about the challenge from Z-Arts to create a learning resource for their Christmas show which will be potentially used by 1700 school pupils. Other challenges which ignited passions were the library project to engage young people and families with John Rylands central library, and the tasks set by Manchester Refugee Support Network.

Whilst over 17 projects have already be proposed to EdLab from outside partners, it was clear that students also had their own ideas and identified some important gaps in what was on offer. Some students were excited to create projects working with disabled young people, and this is a great starting point for developing their own EdLab projects from scratch.


Next Steps…………..

On Wednesday 30th September from 12-1pm we will host our first creative workshop space in the student resource room. During this time we will get to know each other, spark creativity and offer support for starting EdLab projects. The space is open to outside partners and students alike, and we look forward to seeing you all. Come and play, and make your #goodstuff happen!

Help Uncovering Hidden Voices Create a Visual History Resource

Uncovering Voices is a project which aims to build on some of the work of the Hidden Voices community research project which focused on the day-to-day lives, views and experiences of Hulme’s older residents. Hidden Voices was lead by local residents and studied the local area. They found that that isolation and disengagement of the elderly was a big problem locally. 


We are looking to work with local partners to support this challenge. The challenge for us is to come up with information, images and text to help jog memories and provoke discussion. There are many resources open to us in the MMU and central library. We can work together to gather these images and stories to put them onto tablets to bring the get togethers of the community groups.
